A chronicled hurting. An endless waking dark, in the iris-light; knowing not why the silver sword is drawn, but breathing low in curtained quarters. Hurried on, we tread above the sun, the moon, and the apocryphal eclipse. Esoteric mutterings draw our vision North, to the dead and pallid gulf, now parting, widening, and then so gently narrowing---as if assembled by a hasty god---a faint and whispered sound creeping up, up, and in through the silence; and there, the charnel murmur of nameless, for I seldom name, and mummified things that writhe like dying titans in the dust, settles on the cobbled floor. Sepulchres yawn open, and gape, awake and waiting for you to descend. Step into the chasm, and face what awaits you: face that bleeding husk, face that chronicled hurting, that abomination's pain, and face me, your pallid remembrance, a broken husk you left here to rot.
