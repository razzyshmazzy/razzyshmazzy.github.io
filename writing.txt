A chronicled hurting. An endless waking dark, a dream of despair in the iris-light; knowing not why the silver sword is drawn, nor why ephemeral blood darkens the snow, but approaching yet swifter hallowed calamity. Breathing low in curtained quarters---hurry on through the field of snow. Broken and stumbling forward, you smoothly collapse, and silver stardust rains down on you in your silent convulsions. But my palm finds the naked, snow-burnt crevice on your inner elbow, and I drag you through the thick snow; you slowly rise and follow. Hurried on, we tread above the sun, the moon, and the apocryphal eclipse. Esoteric mutterings draw our vision North, to the dead and pallid gulf, now parting, widening, and then so gently narrowing---as if assembled by a hasty god---a faint and whispered sound creeping up, up, and in through the silence; and there, the charnel murmur of nameless, for I seldom name, and mummified things that writhe like dying titans in the dust, settles on the cobbled floor. Sepulchres yawn open, and gape, awake and waiting for your descent. Step into the chasm, into the chaos, and face what awaits you: face that bleeding husk, face that chronicled hurting, face that abomination's pain, and face me, your pallid remembrance, a broken husk you left here to rot.
